# Advanced Structural Deal Architecture: Equity, Severance, and Leverage

When base cash compensation has genuinely reached its terminal limit, the negotiation is not over — it shifts to structural deal architecture. The true, generational wealth in executive roles is rarely found in the heavily taxed base salary; it is forged through equity ownership, performance triggers, and creative institutional leverage.

## Milestone Triggers and Performance Tranches

To bridge the gap between an employer's risk tolerance and a candidate's high financial requirements, introduce **performance-based incentives**.

If the candidate claims they can solve a $10M problem, structure the contract to capture a guaranteed percentage of that upside:

> *"If we hit $100 million in ARR, I receive an additional tranche of stock or a specific cash bonus."*

**Why it works:**
- Signals absolute operational confidence
- Mirrors professional athletic contracts (e.g., Tom Brady's performance-based structures tied to Super Bowl victories)
- Demonstrates "skin in the game" to founders and CEOs
- Founders are significantly more willing to break standard compensation rules when the payout is inextricably linked to guaranteed corporate victory

## Negotiating the "Divorce": Severance and OTE

Amateur candidates negotiate only the "marriage" (signing bonus and starting salary). **Elite executive talent relentlessly negotiates the "divorce."**

Severance protections are critical risk-mitigation tools, particularly in volatile technology, media, and private equity sectors.

**Critical detail:** Ensure severance payouts are calculated based on **On-Target Earnings (OTE)** — which includes projected bonuses, variable compensation, and vested equity — rather than merely base salary. Failing to audit this specific contractual language can result in catastrophic financial losses.

**Case study:** A Chief Revenue Officer nearly lost $90,000 simply because the paperwork was vaguely drafted to reflect only base pay.

Also negotiate: retention bonuses for periods of instability (potential company takeovers or PE buyouts), guaranteeing compensation if the corporate structure dissolves.

### The "We, Not Me" Severance Framing

To secure robust severance without appearing uncommitted to the company's future:

> *"Wouldn't it make us look good to proactively give everyone on the executive leadership team [this severance protection] to show they're safe and committed?"*

This allows the CEO to act as a benevolent hero — the executive secures their own financial parachute under the guise of promoting broader organizational stability.

## Creative Perks and Tax-Advantaged Leverage

When cash and standard equity pools are mathematically exhausted, look toward the corporation's internal tax liabilities and operational write-offs.

**Case study — the Mercedes G-Wagon:** A Chief Marketing Officer was absolutely capped at a $2.4M cash offer. The negotiation successfully pivoted to securing a $350,000 Mercedes G-Wagon. Because a vehicle weighing over 6,000 pounds legally qualifies as a heavy equipment tax write-off for the corporation, the company was able to:
- Bypass strict HR salary budgets entirely
- Provide immense, tangible lifestyle value to the executive
- Simultaneously solve a corporate tax problem

This level of lateral, creative deal-making separates standard management hires from elite enterprise talent.
